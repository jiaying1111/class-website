Alphabetical 2025

About and Me

2025 has felt like a year of transition. As I think about graduation and what comes after, I’ve been paying closer attention to my thoughts, emotions, and working process. This website is a quiet record of that time, collecting moments of uncertainty, focus, rest, and change—organized alphabetically rather than chronologically, as a way to notice how the year actually felt.

Notes / Definitions

A — Anxiety

A recurring state of alertness, tension, and overthinking. Quiet, then sudden.

B — Bold

Moving anyway. Not fearless—just willing.

C — Cry

A release when language arrives too late.

D — Daydream

A soft drift into imagined or remembered spaces.

E — Edge

The point between stability and collapse. Heightened awareness.

F — Fuzzy

Blurred boundaries—thoughts without sharp outlines.

1. Anxiety

Thoughts about graduation, my portfolio, and what comes after school appeared more frequently, sometimes without clear reasons. I remembered there were many 2 a.m. nights when I stared at social media, looking at other people’s lives. What kind of life will I be living in five years?

2. Bold

It showed up in small decisions—continuing to work despite doubt, sharing ideas before they felt fully formed, and choosing to move forward without clear answers. Being bold often meant acting first and understanding later.

3. Cry

I learned not to cry too much this year, though I’m not sure whether that means I became stronger or simply more practiced at holding things in.

4. Daydream

I spent more time sleeping during the day, so it was, quite literally, daydreaming. During the final weeks, I stayed up very late at night. The quiet helped me focus, but at the same time, I hated how empty it felt when it settled around me.

5. Edge

I spent more time focusing on exploring different “edges” in my work. That in-between state feels alive to me—unstable, unfinished, and full of possibility. I love watching forms shift, stretch, and transform as they move across that boundary, as if they are still deciding what they want to become.

6. Fuzzy

Many things felt fuzzy this year. Plans overlapped, emotions blurred, and directions were rarely clear. I often felt like I was moving without a sharp outline of where I was headed. I began to sit with the blur, letting uncertainty exist without immediately resolving it.
